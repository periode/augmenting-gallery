the new museology

natural history museums both objectify and educate. in the past, they used to be teleogical justification of the superiority of the (white).

with a shift from the new museology movement in the 1970s, we can see how they reinvent themselves.

climate change education at nature-based museums

education

one of the Paris agreement's pledges is

to substantially increase public education and participation

and a complex, multi-scalar phenomenon demands a multi-faceted response.

what should visitors be educated to?

  • extinct species, or soon to be extinct (consequences)
  • emotional and motivational response
  • personal responses/actions

Relations between climate change, science, culture, and social practices need to be reframed. Current approaches to representing the science of climate change in museums and science centers are based on a separation of the science from its social and cultural dimensions. Climate change must be embedded in all programs. Climate change as a phenomenon is now part of the ecology of life, and it must be embraced as a fundamental element of living in the contemporary world.

museums' roles also change as public perception of climate change evolves (from cultural construct to scientific agreement)
